Emberglass runs a fine-tuned Qwen2.5-3B reasoning model entirely on your own GPU, from a single static page, in hand-written WebGPU compute shaders. It thinks for thousands of tokens and streams a verdict — and nothing ever leaves your device. You can swap the model's specialization live by hot-swapping a LoRA adapter: the base loads once and never moves. Code: github.com/maceip/qwen-webgpu-lora.

Bring your own model — streamed straight to your GPU. This page hosts no weights. Point it at a Hugging Face repo and it streams the Qwen2.5-3B checkpoint directly from HF into your browser (range requests, quantized to int4 on the way to the GPU) — nothing ever leaves your device. Needs a browser exposing WebGPU with the subgroups feature (Chrome 128+ / Canary). Drag in a PEFT/MLX LoRA adapter to hot-swap a specialization. Project page: huggingface.co/macmacmacmac/qwen-webgpu-lora.
